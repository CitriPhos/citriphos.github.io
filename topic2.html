Genres

House

  • Began in Chicago -> one of the most prominent & popular genres
  • Features a repetitive disco rhythm with percussion made through a drum machin & various percussive instruments for a Latin/soul feel
    Subgenres of House include:
  • Deep House
  • Progressive House
  • Electro House
    Notable artists who contributed to making music in this genre include:
  • David Guetta
  • deadmau5
  • Avicii
  • Daft Punk
  • Zedd

Techno

  • founded by the Belleville Three - Juan Atkins, Derrick May and Kevin Saunderson
  • Emphasis on atmosphere and an often extended buildup - driven by a repetitive, common beat under synthesizers and other electronic gear
  • early media coverage of EDM in the 80s and 90s used "techno" as a catch-all term
      • Notable Artists who contributed to making music in this genre include:
    • Juan Atkins
    • Derrick May
    • Kevin Saunderson
    • Carl Cox

Trance

  • born in Germany - known for building up and breaking down huge melodies
  • started as a subgenre of house - shares traits with techno, new age and synth pop
  • first few tracks emerged in the late 80s -> early 90s is when the genre became more popular
      • Subgenres of Trance include:
    • Progressive Trance
    • Tech Trance
    • Psytrance

    • Ferry Corsten

Dubstep

  • started as a more experimental take on 2-step that was running through London in the late 90s
  • early sound og dubstep are over a decade old - far from the aggressive tracks that are associated with the genre today
  • grew in 2005 with DJs like John Peel and Mary Anne Hobbs bringing it to the UK radio
      • Subgenres of Dubstep include:
    • Brostep
    • Luvstep
    • Thugstep
      Notable Artists who contributed to making music in this genre include:
    • Skrillex
    • Nero
    • Bassnectar
    • Excision

Drum and Bass

  • While its roots were in the hardcore rave scene of London in the early 90s - mutated into a darker sound all to its own
  • includes reggae/dancehall sub bass with sped up breakbeats at 160-180 bpm
  • Has encompassed a no. of sounds with subgenres incorporating jazz, soul, hip-hop and trance
      • Subgenres of DnB include:
    • Drumstep
    • Liquid Funk
    • Techstep
    • Neurofunk
    • Jump-Up
      Notable Artists who contributed to making music in this genre include:
    • Pendulum
    • Noisia
    • Chase & Status
    • Feint
...and many more!
