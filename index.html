All About EDM

What is EDM?

EDM, also known as Electronic Dance Music, refers to an umbrella term for a panoply of musical styles that emerged in the mid-1980s. It consists of various styles ranging from beatless ambient music to hardcore, with house, techno, drum and bass, dubstep and trance. This site aims to educate people about what EDM is, the history and influences surrounding it and artists you should know in the EDM scene.

How did EDM start?

The origins of EDM dates back from the disco genre, which was heavily popular in the late 70s. Since then, Since the 80s with the introduction of synthpop during the "post-disco" era, EDM has since evolved from the music genre we know today.

What are the subgenres?

EDM has created multiple subgenres such as Trance, Electro, House, Dub, Techno, Drum and Bass, etc.

Notable Artists

EDM has created many multiple artists in the music industry such as Avicii, David Guetta, Marshmello, Calvin Harris, Skrillex and deadmau5.

  • Hip Hop has contributed to the heavy influence on EDM; it rose up from an economic crisis in New York

  • In 1979 - The Sugarhill Gang released 'Rapper's Delight' using Chic's popular discor track 'Good Times' - introducing hip hop music to a much wider audience
  • Synthpop was developed alongside house music & electro music during the "post-disco" era in the "80s
  • Music were produced in the mainstream music industry in Europe as EDM becaome more technological advancements
  • MIDI revolutionized the way electronic instruments could communitcate

    • Take On Me - a-ha
    • On and On - Jesse Saunders
  • Electro - greatly inspired by hip hop and German synthpop - electro replaced more traditional instruments such as the bass guitar and the drums with synthesizers & drum machines
  • Afrika Bambaataa's 'Planet Rock' - became synonymous w/ hip hop & electronic music culture and Bambaataa would continue to produce numerous genre-defining electro tracks
  • new subgenres introduced in this period were techno, house, hardcore, dub, trance and drum and bass
  • the rave scene in the UK and Germany shaped the emergence of Trance music, which originated in Germany

  • Detroit techno -> pioneered by the "Belleville Three" including Derrick Mayr
  • May's song "Strings of Life" -> track that defined the techno genre
  • EDM picked up popularity in the US thanks to international DJs & music producers
  • Tiesto, Daft Punk & David Guetta -> have made names for themselves within the mainstream music industry
  • Dubstep was also introduced to the US through artists like Skrillex -> Grammy-winning EDM artist with sounds of dubstep, house, trap and more
  • EDM is often characterized by remixes and original sound mixes produced by world-famous DJs -> it is also used by a majority of pop and hip-hop artists
